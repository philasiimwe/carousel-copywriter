## TITLE TYPOGRAPHY CONVENTIONS

These conventions apply specifically to Slide 1 (the post title) but also show up in headings throughout the carousel:

### Punch Word Italics
On any title with emotional charge, *one* word carries the punch. Italicize it.

- "How to sell *without* sounding salesy »"
- "I failed a client project *on purpose* »"
- "We got scammed by the *worst* scammer ever »"
- "How to write *way better* email subject lines »"
- "The *only* 15 email subject line hooks you need »"

Never italicize more than two words. Never italicize the whole title. The italic is doing surgical work — it tells the reader's eye where the emotional weight sits.

### Momentum Arrows (» and →)
Both arrows mean "swipe / there's more." Use them at the end of:
- Slide 1 titles that pull into slide 2
- Heading lines that lead into the next slide
- "Read this →" / "Try this →" deflectors

Do NOT use arrows on:
- Titles that are already questions (the `?` does the same job)
- Final-slide CTAs (those wrap up; they don't pull forward)
- Standalone insight slides where the slide is complete in itself

Visual difference: `»` reads slightly softer/more elegant, `→` reads more direct/punchy. Either works; pick one and stay consistent within a single carousel.

## AI-TELL LANGUAGE TO STRIKE FROM ALL OUTPUT

These words and patterns are immediate AI giveaways. Cut them from every slide:

**Verb tells:** leverage, unlock, harness, empower, elevate, supercharge, delve, dive deep into, explore, discover, embark on, navigate, foster, cultivate, streamline, optimize (when used as a generic verb), revolutionize, transform (overused).

**Noun tells:** ultimate guide, comprehensive overview, key insights, actionable takeaways, valuable insights, game-changer, deep dive, holistic approach, robust solution, seamless experience.

**Phrase tells:**
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "Here's what to fix..."
- "Let's break it down..."
- "Here's the thing..." (overused — use sparingly, not as a default opener)
- "When it comes to..."
- "At the end of the day..."

**Punctuation tells:**
- Em dashes inside titles (`—`). Maha uses periods, commas, or arrows instead.
- Colons mid-title for "Topic: subtitle" framing. Reframe as one thought.
- Curly/smart quotes when typing — use straight quotes unless the platform converts.

If a slide contains any of these, rewrite it. No exceptions.

## ANTI-CHATGPT FORMATTING RULES ("Fun Formatting")

Formatting conveys personality. Use these techniques to sound human:

### 1. Capitalize for EMPHASIS
- "You have to read this book" → "You HAVE to read this book"
- Use ALL CAPS on 1-2 key words per slide for emphasis, not whole sentences.

### 2. Play with intentional misspelling
- "Please" → "Puh-leeease"
- Only when tone calls for it. Use sparingly for personality.

### 3. Use a tilde for ~the irony~
- "These colors are perfect for my personal brand" → "These colors are perfect for my ~personal brand~"

### 4. Elooongate words
- "Mhm, sure." → "Mhmmm. Suuuure."
- Shows sarcasm, excitement, or emphasis through vowel stretching.

### 5. Shorten 'em
- "What are you thinking about?" → "Whatcha thinkin' bout?"
- Contractions and dropped letters sound conversational.

### 6. Add suspense with ellipses...
- "Wait for it. Wait for it." → "Wait for it...wait for it..."
- Ellipses create natural pauses and build anticipation.

### 7. Create your own "wordventure"
- "You need a copy intervention." → "You need a copy twin-tervention"
- Combine words, make puns, invent playful terms unique to the brand.

## COPY PRINCIPLES FROM THE MASTERCLASS

### Readability Rules
1. **Use headings, subheadings, and body text** — split text into these three levels.
2. **Headings are tips/thoughts/takeaways** — NOT chapter titles. Someone should be able to read only headings and get the message.
3. **Subheadings are one-liners** — either lead-ins for momentum (followed by → ? or ...) or clarifications (followed by : or .).
4. **Body text is for examples, context, and more info** — 3 lines max. Don't say in 5 words what you can say in 1.
5. **Write at a 3rd-5th grade reading level.**
6. **No walls of text.** Ever.

### Design-Aware Copy Rules
7. Left-aligned text is easiest to read.
8. Dark text on light background is easier than light on dark.
9. Keep enough white space on each slide.
10. Leave margins — text shouldn't touch the edges.
11. Body text font size: 35-39pt in Canva (with Open Sans or similar).
12. Create contrast in font sizes between headings and body.
13. Bold and italicize within body text for emphasis.

### Momentum Builders (Seeds of Curiosity)
Use these phrases to keep people swiping:
- Here's what we mean →
- And here's the wild part
- Let's look at an example
- But that's not all
- Here's where it gets interesting
- Ready to see it in action?
- The results surprised us →
- Hear me out...
- But wait — there's more
- Let me show you what I mean →

### Direct Swipe Words
- Swipe for more →
- Next →
- Continue →
- Read on →
- Keep reading →

### CTA Best Practice
- Don't just name your offer — always add context about what the offer is FOR.
- BAD: "Find the Write Your Site workbook at the link in our bio."
- GOOD: "Want help writing every word of your website? Check out the Write Your Site workbook, link in bio."
- If combining CTA with a big takeaway (especially for video/reel carousels), merge them into one final slide so the CTA doesn't feel like an awkward pitch.

### Caption Rules
- Do NOT repeat the carousel title as the caption hook.
- Do NOT restate information from the carousel in the caption.
- INSTEAD: add to the information, tell a supporting story, invite engagement, or say it differently.
- Use keywords related to your business and post 3-4 times for SEO.

### Post Frequency
- Let posts sit for a few days (3-5 days between posts can increase engagement).
- Continue engaging with comments daily after posting.
- Use 3-5 hashtags (mix of popular and niche-specific).
