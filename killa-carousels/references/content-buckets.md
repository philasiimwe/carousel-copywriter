# High-Selling Content Buckets Reference
## By Phil Asiimwe.

---

## EDUCATE BUCKET
*To help people understand the value of your work*

### Problem Awareness
Make your people aware of a problem they're facing, why they're facing it, what's keeping them stuck, why what they've tried hasn't worked before, and what people get wrong. The problem usually ISN'T what people think it is. Focus on how the audience is contributing to the problem or avoiding addressing it.

### Actionable Tips
Give simple steps to help give people clarity on what they need to do to move through a problem. These may be how-tos, tips, step-by-step breakdowns, or tutorials.

### Value Shares
Show your authority by sharing your knowledge. These can be framed as "truths" or "reveals," though they may be baseline knowledge within your industry. Don't create a message based on a statistic — choose your message first, then find the best statistic to illustrate it.

### Plug-and-Play
Do the work for your people. These "must-save" posts feel value-packed because they give away your work for free, even when it's just one tiny piece of a giant puzzle. Group plug-and-play content into categories based on identity for originality.

### Expert Showcase
Lift the curtain on your expertise by showing why what you do is so good. These posts use your own work to explain the "excellence" behind it. Note: This isn't about how you run your business — it's related to your expertise around what you offer.

### Business Insider
Educate your audience on the backend of your business. What resources do you use and love? Why do you do things the way you do them? These posts work well for people who want to be seen as leaders in their profession and lean into business education.

## RELATE BUCKET
*To connect with your people and humanize your business*

### Personal Introductions
Introduce the likable human (or team!) behind the business to make it feel personal. People buy from people!

### Story Shares
Share the (often relatable) moments along your journey of business ownership. This can be moments of struggle, personal transformation, before & afters — you name it!

### Behind The Scenes
Lift the curtain on how the magic happens so people can cheer on the human(s) behind the biz. What do your daily processes look like behind the scenes? What does your day-to-day life look like?

### Engagement Invitations
Create a concrete way for people to engage with you or your content. This can look like questions, games, challenges, or activities. Feel free to get creative!

### Reflections & Recaps
What's going on right now in your business? What's working, and what's not, and WHY? What are you currently learning about/processing/building?

### Just For Fun
Show your personality, your humor, or your personal life through the lens of your business. Emphasis on *through the lens of your business!*

## INSPIRE BUCKET
*To show what's possible when you step in/say "yes"*

### Case Studies
An in-depth study of one specific client/customer. Focus case studies on one specific change: what happened when WE/CLIENT stopped doing X and started doing Y.

### Struggle Shares
Share client "before" stories that make transformations visible and relatable. What did they tell you they were struggling with BEFORE working with you? What hesitations did they have? When did they almost quit? What would they have missed out on had they skipped out on your solution?

### Turning Points
What realizations/big shifts did people need to step into to start seeing results? These should speak to false beliefs/what people get wrong/the solutions you offer.

### Before & Afters
Show the before and after, whether it's visible, quantitative, qualitative, or told through a story.

### Visualizations & Projections
Think of these posts as hypothetical case studies that help people see the value in jumping into buying from you.

### Portfolio Shares
Share the detailed deliverables of a completed offer or product.

## SELL BUCKET
*To share your offers with the people who want/need them*

### Offer Pitch
Connect the problem people are having to an offer. These direct offer shoutouts work best when you're "live launching" and enrollment is only open for a set number of days, OR when there are time-sensitive perks included during the initial "launch."

### The Only Option
Give insight into why your offer includes what it includes — and what it doesn't! What do others get wrong? What do you do better? How does your thing hold up against/stand out from all the other similar things?

### Self-Selection
These posts center around the question "Is this you?" Lead with SPECIFIC pain points and problems your people are facing as a way to self-select into your offer.

### Sliding Door Stories
Stories that help people understand the drawbacks of NOT jumping on this offer and what alternative they will be missing out on/why this is the best option to get what they want.

### Excitement Builders
Giveaways, sweepstakes, challenges, special bonuses — these posts increase excitement around an offer and invite more people to get involved in the promotion.

### Lead Generators
These posts point to freebies, resources, or discounts and are used as either email list builders or DM incentives.

### FAOs (Frequently Asked Objections)
Directly answer frequently asked questions people have related to an OBJECTION they have about your offer. These shouldn't be FAQs about what's included in your offer, but FAObjections like "Is this right for me? What if I don't feel ready? What if it feels like a big investment?" etc.
